What is blight?

Blight is the decay of a community and its resources. The main sign of blight is abandoned buildings and neighborhoods. This fosters the growth crime and lowers property values in the surrounding areas.

About the Unblight Unconference

What is it?

The Unblight Unconference was a conference held in Macon, GA on August 14th and 15th. It was organized by Mercer University's Center for Collaborative Journalism (CCJ) and the Sunlight Foundation

Who attended?

Journalists, policy makers, technologists, students, and Macon citizens interested in ending the blight in their city.

What was the goal?

To bring together a community to form new ideas and ways to combat the blight attacking their area.

Problems Faced in Combating Blight

Demolition is becoming harder and harder according to this article by the Macon Telegraph. Older homes need to be inspected for asbestos and prices for demolition are on the rise. To top it off, even with the city's plan to demolish houses and buildings on the regular, it won't help much because there are buildings being abandoned every day.

What is being done now?

According to the same article by the Macon Telegraph, there is the Five by Five program, there are Economic and Community development loans and grants available, and there exist Shalom Zones, which you can continue reading about in the article link above.
